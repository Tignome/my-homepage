Seasonal Tips (Late April)
  • Warm days, dry season — hat, sunscreen, water bottle.
  • Cash helps at markets; many workshops accept cards.
  • Taxis/rideshare are common; confirm fare before riding.
  • Sunday: Tlacolula market; Mondays: some museums closed.
Bringing Alebrijes Home
  • Small: wrap well and carry on.
  • Medium: double-wrap, box, pad inside checked luggage.
  • Large: ask workshop for insured DHL/FedEx shipping.
  • Declare as “wooden handicraft souvenir” at customs.

            Souvenirs & Shopping Tips

            • Alebrijes — carved/painted copal wood figures. Best in San Martín Tilcajete and San Antonio Arrazola. Look for fine carving, natural-dye detail, and artist signature.
            • Barro negro (black pottery) — from San Bartolo Coyotepec. Choose thicker, even walls and a clean sheen; pack with clothing.
            • Green-glazed pottery — from Santa María Atzompa. Great for tableware; check for stable base and food-safe glazing.
            • Textiles — rugs & rebozos from Teotitlán del Valle. Ask about natural dyes (cochineal, indigo) and hand-loomed work.
            • Oaxacan chocolate — drink tablets/blocks (e.g., Mayordomo). Choose vacuum-sealed packs to travel better.
            • Mole pastes — negro, rojo, coloradito. Buy sealed packets at Mercado Benito Juárez / 20 de Noviembre.
            • Mezcal — artisanal, 100% agave; keep receipts and pack in checked luggage. Tastings in Centro or certified palenques on tours.
            • Coffee — Sierra Norte/Sur beans; prefer recent roast dates and whole beans.
            • Corn goods — totopos, tlayuda tortillas (sealed). Durable and delicious gifts.
            Buying & packing: pay in cash in villages, compare quality, request receipts; for fragile items, double-wrap and carry-on if possible.
